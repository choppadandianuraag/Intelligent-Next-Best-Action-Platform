# Meeting Notes — Globex Corp Q1 Review (Resolved Case)
**Date:** 2026-03-15
**Participants:** Alex Rivera (CSM), Karen Wu (Head of Analytics, Globex Corp), VP Thomas Grant
**Duration:** 60 minutes — Executive Business Review
**Account:** Globex Corp | ARR: $145,000 | Contract End: 2026-12-15
**Status:** RESOLVED — Renewed

---

**Context:** This EBR was triggered after Globex Corp showed declining engagement in Q4 2025. Health score had dropped from 81 to 58 over 3 months. DAU dropped from 52 to 31. Primary champion Karen Wu had flagged uncertainty about ROI during Q4 check-in.

---

**Alex:** Thomas, Karen — thank you for making time for this EBR. I want to start by owning something: we didn't do enough to help you demonstrate ROI during Q4, and your health metrics showed it.

**Thomas:** I appreciate the directness. Karen, take it from here.

**Karen:** Q4 was rough. We had two team changes, we lost two power users, and the reporting module wasn't delivering what we needed. I was honestly questioning whether we'd renew.

**Alex:** I've prepared a full ROI analysis for today. Based on your usage data, here's what the platform delivered in Q1 alone...

*(Alex shares screen with ROI dashboard)*

**Alex:** 47 automated workflows running, 312 hours of manual reporting time eliminated, and three early churn signals detected in your customer base that your ops team acted on.

**Thomas:** 312 hours. What does that translate to in dollars?

**Alex:** At your team's loaded cost rate, approximately $47,000 in recovered productivity — more than half your annual contract value in one quarter.

**Thomas:** That's a compelling number. Karen, is that accurate?

**Karen:** It's directionally right. I hadn't quantified it that way before.

**Alex:** We've also resolved the reporting issues that frustrated Karen's team in Q4. I want to walk through the new reporting module features that shipped in February.

**Karen:** The new drill-down capability is exactly what we were missing. I've already been using it this week.

**Thomas:** Alex, I've heard enough. What does renewal look like?

**Alex:** We'd like to keep you on your current terms and add the advanced analytics add-on — an additional $18K annually. Given the ROI we just walked through, I think that's defensible.

**Thomas:** Let's do it.

---

**Outcome:** Renewed with $18K expansion. Health score recovered to 74 within 6 weeks post-EBR. Karen re-engaged as internal champion. EBR was the inflection point.

**CSM Notes:** EBR format worked: lead with owned accountability, present quantified ROI, demo new features, close. Executive sponsorship from Thomas was critical — ensure exec-level contacts for at-risk accounts.
