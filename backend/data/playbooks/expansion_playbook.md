# Expansion Playbook

## Overview
Expansion is the most capital-efficient growth lever in SaaS. A 5% improvement in net revenue retention is worth more than a 5% improvement in new logo acquisition. This playbook defines the signals that indicate expansion readiness and the conversation framework for upselling and cross-selling.

## Expansion Readiness Signals

| Signal | Strength | Action |
|--------|----------|--------|
| DAU consistently >70% of licensed seats | Strong | Initiate seat expansion conversation |
| Power users requesting features on higher tier | Strong | Schedule upgrade discovery call |
| Customer asks about API access | Strong | Present enterprise tier |
| Customer mentions new team or department | Medium | Map new team's use case |
| NPS >60 at 90+ days | Medium | Request referral + expansion conversation |
| Successful QBR with positive outcome | Medium | Introduce expansion roadmap |
| Active automation usage >10 workflows | Medium | Suggest automation add-on |
| Approaching data volume limits | Strong | Proactively flag before they notice |

## Expansion Conversation Framework

### Step 1 — Anchor on Current Value
Start the expansion conversation by recapping documented ROI: "You've saved X hours, caught Y issues, accelerated Z decisions. Here's what that looks like quantified."

### Step 2 — Surface the Gap
Identify what they can't do today: "I've noticed your team is approaching the seat limit / you mentioned wanting API access / you have three new teams joining in Q3."

### Step 3 — Paint the Expanded Future
Show — don't just describe — what the next tier enables. Use a live demo or screen share. Connect every feature to a specific outcome the customer has stated.

### Step 4 — ROI Bridge
Quantify the expansion investment against expected return. Example: "Enterprise tier is an additional $65K annually. Based on what you've told me about your data engineering time, the Snowflake connector alone saves 3–4 hours/week — at your team's rate, that's $40–50K in recovered productivity."

### Step 5 — Create Internal Champion Momentum
Identify who needs to approve the expansion (CFO, VP, CEO?) and equip the day-to-day contact to make the case internally. Send a one-page business case document.

### Step 6 — Define Clear Next Steps
Never leave without a specific date for the next conversation. "I'll send the proposal today — can we reconnect Thursday to walk through it with your VP?"

## Pricing Conversation Guidelines
- Never quote pricing in the same breath as features — build value first
- Always position expansion as investment vs. cost
- For multi-team expansions, offer a phased rollout to reduce budget friction
- Present the ROI math before the price
- If price objection: "What would we need to show you to make this an easy yes?"

## Common Expansion Mistakes
- Pitching expansion before 60-day milestone achievement
- Focusing on features instead of outcomes
- Not having economic buyer in the room for pricing conversation
- Assuming current contact has budget authority
- Discounting too early — it devalues the product and trains customers to wait
