# Onboarding Best Practices

## Overview
Successful onboarding is the single greatest predictor of long-term retention. Accounts that reach key milestones within 30 days have 2.3× higher renewal rates. This playbook defines the 30/60/90 day framework for new customer onboarding.

## 30-Day Milestones (Technical Foundation)
- [ ] SSO configured and all invited users can log in
- [ ] Primary data connector live and syncing correctly
- [ ] At least 5 custom alert thresholds configured
- [ ] At least 3 users have completed the in-app guided tour
- [ ] First custom dashboard created
- [ ] CSM check-in call completed at week 2

**Target DAU at day 30:** 40% of licensed seats

## 60-Day Milestones (Value Realization)
- [ ] At least 2 automation workflows active
- [ ] First custom report generated and shared internally
- [ ] Reporting module configured with at least 3 metrics
- [ ] Internal champion identified and engaged
- [ ] Use case documented: "What problem does this solve for us?"
- [ ] CSM health check call completed at week 6

**Target DAU at day 60:** 60% of licensed seats

## 90-Day Milestones (Habit Formation)
- [ ] Automation workflows running at cadence (daily/weekly triggers)
- [ ] At least one cross-team collaboration workflow active
- [ ] Success plan reviewed and updated with Q2 goals
- [ ] Executive sponsor has seen a platform demo or summary
- [ ] NPS survey sent — target score >50

**Target DAU at day 90:** 70%+ of licensed seats

## Warning Signs During Onboarding
- DAU below 20% of seats at day 14: schedule intervention immediately
- No automation workflows at day 45: re-onboarding session needed
- Jira/SSO/connector issues unresolved after 7 days: escalate to P1
- No exec sponsor identified by day 30: escalate within CSM team
- NPS below 40 at 90-day mark: flag for churn risk review

## Key Tactics

### Week 1 — Quick Win Focus
Get users to see value within the first 5 logins. The alert feature is typically the fastest path to a quick win — help customers configure 3 meaningful alerts in the first session.

### Week 2 — Expansion Within Team
Identify power users (top 3 most active) and invest in them. They become internal advocates. Ask them: "Who else on your team would benefit from seeing this?"

### Week 3–4 — Automation Introduction
Manual work + automation demo is the most effective framing. Show the customer what task they do manually today, then show how automation handles it. Record the session.

### Month 2 — Integration Deepening
Connect secondary data sources. Each new connector increases stickiness significantly (data portability friction increases switching cost).

### Month 3 — Executive Touchpoint
Get 15 minutes with the economic buyer. Present a simple ROI slide: hours saved, errors caught, decisions accelerated.
