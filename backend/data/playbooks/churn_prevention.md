# Churn Prevention Playbook

## Overview
Churn prevention is the highest-priority activity in customer success. Early, proactive intervention is 3–5× more effective than last-minute save attempts. This playbook defines the signals, intervention ladder, and timelines for preventing involuntary and voluntary churn.

## Risk Signal Table

| Signal | Severity | Action Required Within |
|--------|----------|------------------------|
| DAU drops >30% week-over-week | High | 48 hours |
| No login from key users >10 days | High | 24 hours |
| Support tickets spike >2× baseline | High | 48 hours |
| NPS drops >20 points | High | 48 hours |
| Explicit churn threat in call/email | Critical | Same day |
| Competitor evaluation mentioned | High | 24 hours |
| Champion departure confirmed | High | 24 hours |
| Contract renewal <60 days, no renewal discussion | Medium | 1 week |
| Feature adoption stalled at <30% | Medium | 1 week |
| Silence from account >14 days | Medium | 3 days |
| Open support tickets >5 unresolved | Medium | 3 days |

## Intervention Ladder

### Level 1 — Proactive Outreach (Risk: Medium)
- Personalized email with usage insights
- Offer a check-in call within the week
- Share relevant product updates or case studies
- Timeline: Act within 1 week

### Level 2 — Structured Check-in (Risk: Medium-High)
- Schedule 30-minute video call with decision maker
- Prepare custom health report
- Identify and address adoption gaps
- Timeline: Act within 3 days

### Level 3 — Executive Business Review (Risk: High)
- Schedule EBR with exec sponsor within 2 weeks
- Prepare ROI analysis with hard numbers
- Bring CSM manager or VP to the call
- Present concrete action plan with commitments
- Timeline: Act within 1 week of escalation flag

### Level 4 — Critical Account Save (Risk: Critical)
- Immediate escalation to VP Customer Success
- Same-day outreach from leadership
- All open tickets escalated to P0
- Dedicated support pod assigned
- Executive sponsor engaged on vendor side
- Weekly check-ins until stabilized
- Timeline: Act same day

## Key Timelines
- Health score recovery typically takes 6–8 weeks post-intervention
- EBR-to-renewal decisions usually close within 14 days of EBR
- Re-onboarding recovery: 8–10 weeks for full DAU restoration
- Champion change recovery: 4–6 weeks for new champion enablement

## Common Mistakes to Avoid
- Waiting for the customer to raise the issue (they rarely do until it's too late)
- Sending documentation instead of scheduling live calls
- Missing the executive sponsor when the day-to-day contact is at risk
- Offering discounts before understanding root cause
- Treating churn risk as a one-time event instead of an ongoing process
