# Competitive Positioning Guide

## Overview
Competitive evaluations are normal. The goal is to articulate differentiated value — not disparage competitors.

## Key Competitors

### Gainsight
**Weakness:** $200K+ entry, 6-12 month implementation, steep learning curve.
**Our positioning:** "Same core CS functionality at 40-60% of the cost. Median time-to-value: 14 days vs. Gainsight's 90-180 days."

### Salesforce Success Cloud
**Weakness:** Requires Salesforce as prerequisite, limited automation, expensive combined licensing.
**Our positioning:** "The moment you have non-Salesforce systems (Jira, HubSpot, Snowflake), you're building custom integrations. We cover 30+ tools natively."

### Totango
**Weakness:** Limited automation, weaker reporting, small ecosystem.
**Our positioning:** "Strong health scoring but limited in automation depth. Our automation module runs 10+ step conditional workflows out of the box."

### ChurnZero
**Weakness:** No native data warehouse integration, weak API.
**Our positioning:** "For data-forward teams wanting Snowflake/BigQuery pipelines and custom Tableau dashboards — our API-first design is the differentiator."

## Handling an Active Competitive Evaluation

1. Understand the driver: features, price, or internal politics?
2. Ask for their evaluation criteria explicitly
3. Focus on your top 3 differentiated strengths, not every category
4. Request a 2-week parallel POC
5. Escalate to CSM Manager immediately

## Key Objection Responses

**"Gainsight showed better ROI dashboards."**
"Show me specifically what you need that you can't see today. Our reporting module can be configured to match most of it."

**"It's cheaper to use Salesforce CS."**
"What does your non-Salesforce stack look like? Jira, HubSpot, Snowflake? Custom integration work costs more than licensing savings."

**"We need enterprise features."**
"Which specific features? In my experience 70% of enterprise requests are available on our Growth tier. Let's go through them."
