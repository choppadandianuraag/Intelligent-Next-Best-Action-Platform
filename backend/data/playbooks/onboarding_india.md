# Onboarding Best Practices — India

## Overview
Successful onboarding in India requires adapting to the local work culture — team-based learning, high preference for in-person or video sessions over documentation, and a need to demonstrate early wins within the first 30 days.

## 30 / 60 / 90 Day Onboarding Milestones

### Days 1–30: Foundation
**Goals:** Platform accessible to all users, core workflow set up, first team trained

Checklist:
- [ ] SSO/SAML integration completed (critical — blocks adoption if delayed)
- [ ] All licensed users provisioned and can log in
- [ ] Admin user trained and can manage the platform independently
- [ ] Core dashboard configured for team's specific KPIs
- [ ] First live training session completed (prefer video call or in-person)
- [ ] Slack/Teams support channel established

**Indian Market Note:** SSO integrations frequently get delayed due to IT security review processes. Escalate proactively at Day 7 if not completed. Offer to join an IT call to unblock.

### Days 31–60: Adoption
**Goals:** > 60% of licensed users active weekly, 3+ features in regular use

Checklist:
- [ ] Second training session for non-attending users from session 1
- [ ] At least 2 team-specific workflows configured
- [ ] First usage report shared with champion and their manager
- [ ] Any integration (CRM, ERP, BI tool) completed or on timeline
- [ ] Quick win identified and achieved — document it for the champion
- [ ] First informal check-in: "What's working? What's not?"

**Indian Market Note:** Always share the usage report with the champion's manager. It gives the champion visibility with their leadership and creates internal advocacy for the platform.

### Days 61–90: Value Realisation
**Goals:** > 75% active users, quantified ROI for first QBR, renewal confidence established

Checklist:
- [ ] First QBR conducted
- [ ] ROI quantified: hours saved, errors reduced, manual work eliminated
- [ ] Champion identified at least one team member who is a "power user"
- [ ] Expansion conversation initiated (additional teams, features, or users)
- [ ] Customer satisfaction check (NPS or CSAT survey)
- [ ] Success plan for next 6 months documented and shared

## Training Best Practices for Indian Teams

### Format
- **Preferred:** Live video session (Zoom/Teams/Google Meet) — 60–90 minutes max
- **Second best:** In-person at customer's office for accounts > ₹50L ARR
- **Avoid:** Long documentation or async video recordings as primary training method

### Session Structure
1. **Context setting (10 min):** "Here is what your team will be able to do after this session"
2. **Live demo (30 min):** Show the exact workflow they will use — use their data if possible
3. **Hands-on practice (20 min):** Let them do it while you watch and guide
4. **Q&A (15 min):** Open floor
5. **Next steps (5 min):** What they should try before the next session

### Language
- Sessions in English are standard for metro city enterprise accounts
- For accounts in smaller cities or with mixed-language teams, offer to have a bilingual team member on the call
- Follow-up summaries via WhatsApp in simple, clear English get better read rates than detailed emails

## Common Onboarding Failure Modes in India

| Failure Mode | Cause | Prevention |
|-------------|-------|-----------|
| SSO never completed | IT security review backlog | Escalate at Day 7, offer to join IT call |
| Only champion trained, team not onboarded | Champion assumed self-serve would work | Schedule team training as part of kickoff agreement |
| Platform used only by analytics team | Business team not included in training | Map all user personas in kickoff and train each group |
| Champion leaves before 90 days | High attrition | Identify backup champion in Week 1 |
| No quick win in 30 days | Generic onboarding, not workflow-specific | Customise training to their exact workflows from Day 1 |
