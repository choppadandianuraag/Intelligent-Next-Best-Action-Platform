# Executive Business Review (EBR) Guide

## When to Call an EBR
Schedule an EBR when any of the following are true:
- Account health score drops below 55
- Renewal is within 90 days and risk level is medium or above
- Explicit churn threat or competitor evaluation active
- Champion departure has occurred
- Significant product adoption regression (>25% DAU drop)
- You need exec-level buy-in for expansion

Do NOT wait for a quarterly cadence to trigger an EBR if risk signals are present.

## Participants Required
- **CSM** (always)
- **Customer: Executive Sponsor** (required — decision maker)
- **Customer: Day-to-day contact** (recommended)
- **CSM Manager or VP** (required for critical accounts)
- **Solutions Engineer** (if technical issues are central)

## Agenda Template (60 minutes)

### 1. Open with Accountability (5 min)
- Acknowledge any service gaps or failures on your side
- State clearly what you've committed to fix
- Do not start with a product pitch

### 2. Business Outcomes Review (15 min)
- Present quantified ROI since last review
- Show metrics the customer cares about: time saved, errors reduced, revenue influenced
- Use customer's own data — pull from usage analytics

### 3. Platform Health + Adoption (10 min)
- DAU trends, feature adoption heatmap
- Show what's working and what isn't
- Be honest about gaps

### 4. Roadmap Alignment (15 min)
- Share relevant upcoming features
- Ask: what does success look like for you in the next 6 months?
- Map roadmap items to their stated goals

### 5. Action Plan + Commitments (10 min)
- Specific commitments with named owners and dates
- Next steps agreed verbally and confirmed in writing within 24h

### 6. Renewal Discussion (5 min)
- For renewal accounts: present renewal options
- Get verbal signal on intent
- If expansion opportunity, introduce it here

## EBR Success Metrics
- Executive sponsor re-engaged: Yes/No
- Written action plan sent within 24h: Yes/No
- Health score improvement within 30 days: target +10 points
- Renewal intent confirmed: Yes/No

## Common EBR Mistakes
- Starting with a product demo (leads with features, not outcomes)
- Not having the exec sponsor present (day-to-day contact cannot make renewal decision)
- Failing to send written follow-up within 24 hours
- Presenting data without connecting it to the customer's business goals
- Waiting too long — EBR called <30 days before renewal is often too late
