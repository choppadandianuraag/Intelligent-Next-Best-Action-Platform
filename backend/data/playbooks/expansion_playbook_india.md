# Expansion Playbook — India Market

## Overview
India's enterprise SaaS expansion opportunities are significant but require different approaches than Western markets. Key drivers include multi-city rollouts, business unit consolidation, and API/integration needs from tech-forward companies.

## Expansion Signals to Watch

| Signal | Opportunity | Action |
|--------|-------------|--------|
| Account expanding to new cities or offices | User seat expansion | Proactive enterprise tier proposal |
| Developer/engineering team inquiring about API | API tier upgrade | Technical workshop + scoping call |
| Manual workflows identified in conversation | Automation module upsell | Live demo of automation features |
| Multiple teams on separate contracts | Contract consolidation | Single enterprise agreement |
| Power users asking for admin features | Admin/enterprise tier | Feature walkthrough |
| NPS > 60 AND > 85% adoption | Reference + case study | Partnership conversation |
| Champion mentioning board or leadership interest | Executive partnership | Co-marketing or speaking opportunity |

## Common Expansion Paths in India

### Path 1: City/Office Expansion
Indian enterprises often pilot in one office (typically HQ) and expand to regional offices after proven success. Common expansion cities:
- Tier 1: Mumbai, Bengaluru, Delhi NCR, Chennai, Hyderabad, Pune
- Tier 2: Ahmedabad, Kolkata, Jaipur, Chandigarh, Kochi

**Approach:** Present adoption data from existing office. Calculate per-user cost reduction at scale. Offer volume discount for multi-office deal.

### Path 2: Business Unit Expansion
Large conglomerates (Tata, Mahindra, Reliance, Aditya Birla group companies) have multiple independent business units. A success in one BU opens doors to others.

**Approach:** Request a warm introduction to sister BU. Prepare a one-page "internal case study" the champion can share internally. Incentivise champion with recognition (invite to advisory board, feature in case study).

### Path 3: API & Integration Tier
Tech-forward Indian companies (FinTech, EdTech, D2C, logistics) want to build on top of the platform. The API tier is a premium product they are willing to pay for.

**Approach:** Identify if they have an engineering team actively building integrations. Offer a technical scoping call with your solutions architect. For SAP/Oracle integrations, use the existing connector and demo the setup time (< 2 weeks).

## Expansion Conversation Framework

1. **Confirm current value** — "You mentioned the automation saved 2,200 hours this quarter. How has that changed the way your team works?"
2. **Explore natural next step** — "Where is the next area of friction for your team?"
3. **Connect to their goals** — "You mentioned the Pune team is going to grow — what would it mean for them to have the same visibility your Mumbai team has?"
4. **Present the option** — "We have an enterprise agreement structure that covers all of this under a single commercial. Let me show you what that looks like."
5. **Handle the finance conversation** — Always get the champion on your side before the finance conversation. They need to sponsor the internal approval.

## India Pricing Considerations
- Enterprise discounts are expected for multi-year or multi-city deals — build in 8–12% for negotiation room
- Rupee-based invoicing strongly preferred for Indian subsidiaries of global companies
- GST-compliant invoicing is mandatory — ensure finance team is looped in early
- Payment terms: Net-30 is standard; some PSUs require Net-45 or Net-60
