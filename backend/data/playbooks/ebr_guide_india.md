# Executive Business Review (EBR) Guide — India

## When to Call an EBR

An EBR is the most powerful tool in the CS arsenal, but it must be deployed at the right moment. Call an EBR when:

- Renewal is within 60 days AND health score is below 55
- A new C-suite or VP-level decision maker joins the account
- A major support failure has damaged the relationship
- A competitor is actively in evaluation
- ARR expansion > ₹25L is on the table

**Do not wait for the customer to ask for an EBR.** Proactively propose it.

## Preparation Checklist

### 2 Weeks Before
- [ ] Confirm attendees: customer exec + your VP/Director of CS
- [ ] Pull usage report: DAU trend, features adopted, sessions by team
- [ ] Prepare ROI summary: quantify time saved, errors reduced, process improvements
- [ ] Review all open tickets and resolve or create a resolution timeline
- [ ] Identify 2–3 expansion opportunities to present
- [ ] Prepare a 12-month success roadmap tailored to their goals

### 1 Week Before
- [ ] Share agenda with customer (keep it collaborative — ask what they want to cover)
- [ ] Brief your VP on account history, risks, and talking points
- [ ] Prepare competitive differentiation slides if competitor is in picture
- [ ] If in-person: book venue, confirm travel, send calendar invite with video link as backup

### Day Before
- [ ] Rehearse opening and ROI presentation
- [ ] Review customer's recent news (funding, product launches, headcount changes)
- [ ] Confirm attendance

## EBR Agenda Template (60 minutes)

| Time | Section | Owner |
|------|---------|-------|
| 0–5 min | Welcome and introductions | CSM |
| 5–15 min | What we heard — recap of challenges raised | CSM |
| 15–25 min | Value delivered — ROI, metrics, wins | CSM |
| 25–35 min | Roadmap alignment — what's coming that matters to them | VP/Product |
| 35–45 min | Open discussion — what's not working, what could be better | Customer-led |
| 45–55 min | Next 12 months — success plan and mutual commitments | CSM + Customer |
| 55–60 min | Wrap up and action items | CSM |

## India-Specific EBR Tips

- **Start with chai/coffee small talk** — 5 minutes of personal warmth before business is expected and builds trust
- **Present in-person when possible** — especially for accounts in Mumbai, Bengaluru, Hyderabad, Chennai, Pune, Delhi NCR
- **Bring the ROI in rupees, not percentages** — "₹18 lakh saved in Q1" lands better than "12% efficiency gain"
- **Avoid scheduling during:** Diwali week, Holi week, Eid (for accounts in certain regions), India-Pakistan cricket matches (yes, seriously)
- **Send a WhatsApp summary** after the call with 3 bullet points — it gets read, email sometimes does not

## Success Metrics for EBR
- Customer exec confirms renewal intent verbally
- Open tickets have clear resolution timeline agreed
- At least one expansion conversation initiated
- Next EBR or QBR scheduled before leaving the call
