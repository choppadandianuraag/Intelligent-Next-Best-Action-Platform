# Champion Change Playbook — India

## Overview
Champion attrition is one of the highest-risk events in Indian enterprise accounts. India's job market has extremely high mobility — average tenure for mid-level tech professionals is 18–24 months. A champion leaving can trigger a renewal risk within weeks.

## Detecting Champion Change Early

Watch for these signals:
- Champion's LinkedIn shows "Open to Work" or a new company update
- Reduced response rate from champion over 2+ weeks
- New name CC'd on email threads
- Out-of-office reply with "I have moved on" or similar
- HR/admin contact requesting account access changes

**Action:** Do not wait for confirmation. Reach out warmly as soon as you notice reduced engagement.

## Immediate Response (Days 1–5)

1. **Reach out to departing champion** (if confirmed): Thank them for the partnership. Ask if they are moving to a company that might benefit from the platform (future opportunity). Ask for a warm introduction to their successor.

2. **Contact the account:** "Hi [backup contact], I wanted to check in since I know [champion name] is transitioning. I want to make sure there is no disruption to your team's work on the platform. Who should I connect with going forward?"

3. **Map the new stakeholder landscape:** Who is the new champion? Who is the economic buyer? Is there an executive sponsor?

4. **Brief your VP:** Champion changes that coincide with renewal windows within 90 days should be escalated immediately.

## New Champion Enablement (Weeks 1–3)

### Week 1 — Discovery
- Introduction call: understand new champion's background, priorities, how they prefer to work
- Do NOT assume they know anything about the platform — start fresh
- Ask: "What would make you feel confident about this platform in the next 30 days?"

### Week 2 — Tailored Re-Onboarding
- 90-minute deep-dive session focused on their team's specific workflows
- Skip generic features — show only what is directly relevant to their role
- Assign a dedicated onboarding checklist with their name on it
- Set up a shared Slack/Teams channel for quick support

### Week 3 — Value Anchoring
- Present a usage and ROI summary of what the previous team achieved
- Run a "quick win" exercise — identify one thing the new champion can improve using the platform within 2 weeks
- Schedule their first QBR date

## India-Specific Considerations

### Notice Periods
Indian companies typically have 1–3 month notice periods (much longer than Western markets). This means you often have time to meet the departing champion before they leave and get a proper handover.

**Take advantage of this:** Schedule a joint call with departing champion + successor + yourself for a structured knowledge transfer.

### Relationship with New Champion
New champions in India often feel pressure to demonstrate their own value early. Position the platform as a tool that will help them look good to their leadership. Examples:
- "This dashboard will help you give your VP a real-time view of team performance without a single manual report"
- "Your predecessor used this to cut the monthly ops review prep from 3 days to 4 hours"

### Seniority Shift
Sometimes the departing champion is replaced by someone more senior (promotion opportunity) or more junior (budget cut). Adapt accordingly:
- **More senior:** Shift to executive-level value conversations, ROI framing, strategic roadmap alignment
- **More junior:** More hands-on training, be patient, establish you as a trusted resource they can call

## Success Criteria
- New champion trained and has run at least one workflow independently within 2 weeks
- New champion has been introduced to their manager as a platform advocate
- Renewal not delayed due to champion change
- QBR scheduled within 45 days of champion transition
