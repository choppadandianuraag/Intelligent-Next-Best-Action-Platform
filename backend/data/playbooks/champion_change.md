# Champion Change Playbook

## Overview
A champion change is one of the highest-risk events in a customer relationship. The internal champion — the person who advocated for your product, trained their team, and built workflows around it — leaving the company can unravel months of adoption progress. This playbook defines how to respond within 24 hours and rebuild internal advocacy.

## Immediate Response (0–24 hours)

### Step 1: Confirm the Departure
- Source: LinkedIn update, out-of-office bounce, team member mention in call
- Do NOT assume — confirm with the account before acting on rumors

### Step 2: Identify the Successor
Ask: "Who is taking over [champion's] responsibilities?" or "Is there someone we should loop in to make sure there's continuity?"
- Likely candidates: their direct report, their manager, a peer who used the platform heavily

### Step 3: Outreach to Remaining Contacts
Same-day email to day-to-day contacts:
- Acknowledge the change
- Offer continuity support
- Request a call within 48 hours

### Step 4: Escalate Internally
- Log the champion change in CRM
- Flag account for increased monitoring (weekly health score review)
- Notify CSM Manager
- If renewal is <90 days: escalate to P1

## Re-enablement Protocol (Week 1–4)

### Week 1 — Discovery
- Meet with the incoming champion (or interim contact)
- Understand their familiarity with the platform: zero, some, deep?
- Identify their top 3 pain points/goals
- Do NOT assume they want to use the platform the same way as their predecessor

### Week 2 — Personalized Onboarding
- Run a 1:1 onboarding session tailored to the new champion's goals
- Share the previous champion's top workflows (export as a handoff document)
- Identify 2–3 quick wins specific to the new champion's role

### Week 3–4 — Embedding and Advocacy Building
- Introduce the new champion to your customer community or user group
- Offer co-authored case study or success story (builds their career capital)
- Set up a cadence call with you and the new champion monthly

## Risk Mitigation Strategies
- **Multi-threaded relationships:** Never have a single champion. Invest in 2–3 internal advocates across different levels.
- **Executive sponsor:** Ensure the exec sponsor is independent of the day-to-day champion
- **Documentation:** Encourage champions to document their key workflows — this survives them leaving
- **Admin permissions:** Ensure at least 2 internal admins are configured at all times

## Signals That Champion Change Is Going Badly
- New contact doesn't respond within 5 business days: escalate
- DAU drops >20% within 2 weeks: intervention needed
- New contact says "we're evaluating whether we still need this": P1 escalation
- Support tickets spike after departure: prioritize resolution above all else
