# Renewal Risk Signals — India

## Critical Risk Signals (Act Within 24–48 Hours)

### Signal: Budget Rationalisation Mandate
**What it looks like:** Champion mentions a "cost optimisation exercise", "SaaS audit", or "30% cost reduction target"
**Why it matters:** Indian companies frequently run mid-year and end-of-year budget rationalisation. If your platform is not in the "essential" bucket, it will be cut.
**Action:** Immediately prepare an ROI report in rupees. Schedule a call with the economic buyer (not just the champion). Explore multi-year pricing that provides budget certainty.

### Signal: Silence > 10 Business Days
**What it looks like:** Emails go unanswered, meetings cancelled, no WhatsApp response
**Why it matters:** Indian professionals are generally responsive. Silence often means political activity is happening — a decision is being made without you.
**Action:** Do not send more emails. Call the mobile number directly. If no answer, call the backup contact. Escalate to your VP same day.

### Signal: New Decision Maker (CTO/CFO/VP)
**What it looks like:** New name on email chain, champion says "I need to check with my new manager"
**Why it matters:** New leaders in India often review all inherited vendor relationships in their first 90 days — especially if they came from a company that used a different tool.
**Action:** Request an introduction call within 5 business days. Brief your own VP to reach out peer-to-peer if senior enough. Bring a tailored "welcome" briefing — not a sales pitch.

### Signal: Competitor Mentioned by Name
**What it looks like:** "We are also looking at [competitor]", "They gave us a very good demo last week"
**Why it matters:** If a competitor has already given a demo, you are in a competitive situation. The customer is often looking for a reason to stay, not leave — give them one.
**Action:** Same-day call to understand what the competitor promised. Schedule an EBR within the week. Do not bad-mouth the competitor — demonstrate your own differentiated value.

### Signal: Data Export / Integration Failure
**What it looks like:** Silent data sync failures, export errors, broken CRM/ERP integration
**Why it matters:** Indian enterprise clients often connect the platform to critical internal systems. A broken integration is a productivity blocker that reflects badly on the platform regardless of other features working fine.
**Action:** Treat as P0. Get engineering involved same day. Daily status updates until resolved. No excuses accepted if the failure has been known for > 48 hours without resolution.

## Medium Risk Signals (Act Within 1 Week)

- Health score declining for 3 consecutive months
- DAU below 40% of licensed users
- Open ticket count exceeds 6 simultaneously
- Champion's job title changes (promotion or lateral move — they may lose platform ownership)
- Account's company announces layoffs or restructuring
- NPS drops > 15 points in one survey cycle
- Platform adoption confined to one team (should be spreading to others)

## Low Risk / Early Warning Signals

- No login from 3 or more users in 2 weeks
- Champion misses 2 consecutive scheduled calls
- Feature requests submitted but no acknowledgement sent
- Account not using a module they specifically requested during sales
- Usage concentrated in 1–2 power users, rest of team inactive

## India Calendar — High Risk Periods

| Period | Risk | Reason |
|--------|------|--------|
| January–February | High | Mid-year budget reviews, SaaS audits |
| March | Critical | Financial year end — cost cuts happen fast |
| July–August | Medium | Post-Q1 business reviews |
| October–November | Low | Festive season — decisions slow down |
| December | Medium | Calendar year-end for MNCs operating in India |
