# Churn Prevention Playbook — India Market

## Overview
Indian enterprise accounts have distinct churn patterns shaped by budget cycles (April–March fiscal year), procurement committee structures, and strong relationship-driven decision-making. This playbook covers the key intervention points specific to the Indian market.

## Risk Signal Table

| Signal | Severity | Action Required |
|--------|----------|----------------|
| Renewal within 45 days, health score below 50 | Critical | EBR within 48 hours |
| Decision maker changed (new CTO/VP/CFO) | Critical | Executive introduction call within 5 days |
| Budget cut mandate announced (company-wide) | High | ROI report + pricing flexibility conversation |
| Support tickets > 8 open simultaneously | High | Dedicated support escalation |
| Champion on leave during renewal period | High | Identify and enable backup champion |
| Login activity < 40% of licensed users | Medium | Re-onboarding offer |
| Competitor mentioned by name | Critical | Competitive differentiation call + EBR |
| Silence > 10 business days from primary contact | High | Phone call (not email) to contact |
| NPS dropped > 15 points in one quarter | Medium | Root cause survey + CSM call |

## India-Specific Considerations

### Fiscal Year Timing
Indian companies run April–March fiscal years. Budget reviews and SaaS rationalisation exercises happen in:
- **January–February:** Mid-year review, cost optimisation discussions
- **March:** End-of-year budget flush or cuts
- **April–May:** New budget, new approvals — good time for expansions

### Decision-Making Structure
Large Indian enterprises (TCS, Infosys, Wipro, HCL, Mahindra) have multi-level approval structures. A VP-level champion may not have final authority. Always map:
- **Champion:** Day-to-day user and internal advocate
- **Economic buyer:** Finance sign-off (CFO, Finance Controller)
- **Executive sponsor:** CTO, CIO, or business unit head who can override committee

### Communication Preferences
- WhatsApp is widely used for quick follow-ups — use it for brief status updates after formal calls
- Hindi language outreach appreciated for accounts outside major metros
- In-person visits carry significant relationship weight — prioritise for accounts > ₹50L ARR

## Intervention Ladder

### Level 1 — Proactive (health score 60–75)
- Monthly check-in call
- Share usage insights and feature recommendations
- Send relevant industry benchmarks

### Level 2 — Reactive (health score 45–60)
- Bi-weekly calls
- Root cause analysis of adoption gaps
- Training refresh offer
- Involve Solutions team for technical issues

### Level 3 — At-Risk (health score < 45 or renewal < 60 days)
- Weekly touchpoints
- EBR with executive sponsor
- Formal success plan with 30/60/90 milestones
- Escalate to CSM Manager if no progress in 7 days

### Level 4 — Save (renewal < 30 days, active churn signal)
- Emergency EBR with VP or C-suite
- Customised commercial offer (multi-year, discount, credits)
- Executive sponsor involvement from vendor side
- Last-resort: limited pilot extension to buy time
